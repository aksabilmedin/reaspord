Who is Adar Poonawalla?

Adar Poonawalla, born on January 14, 1981, is a prominent Indian entrepreneur and the CEO of Serum Institute of India. He is known for his leadership in the biotechnology sector and his significant contributions during public health crises. Adar completed his education at the London School of Economics, where he honed his skills in management and business. Under his guidance, Serum Institute has expanded its portfolio and played a crucial role in vaccine development.

What is the History of the Poonawalla Family?

The Poonawalla family's history is deeply intertwined with the growth of the Indian vaccine industry. Cyrus Poonawalla established the Serum Institute of India in 1966 with the vision of making vaccines accessible and affordable to all. His passion for public health and commitment to quality laid the foundation for a successful enterprise that has saved millions of lives. Over the years, the company has expanded its reach, producing vaccines for diseases like polio, measles, and now COVID-19.

Who are the Notable Members of the Adar Poonawalla Family Tree?

The Adar Poonawalla family tree includes several influential figures beyond Adar himself. Key members include:

  • Cyrus Poonawalla: The patriarch and founder of the Serum Institute of India.
  • Natasha Poonawalla: Adar's wife, known for her philanthropic efforts and involvement in various social initiatives.
  • Rahul Poonawalla: Adar's brother, who has contributed to the family's business initiatives.
  • Vikram Poonawalla: Adar's cousin, who plays a role in the family's ventures.
