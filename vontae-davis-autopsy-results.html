What is Chronic Traumatic Encephalopathy (CTE)?

Chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE) is a serious condition that has garnered attention in recent years, particularly in relation to contact sports like football. CTE results from repeated head injuries, leading to a buildup of tau protein in the brain. The condition can cause a range of symptoms, including:

  • Cognitive decline and memory loss.
  • Emotional instability, including depression and anxiety.
  • Impulsivity and aggression.
  • Motor function issues.
